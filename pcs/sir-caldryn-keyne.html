Sir Caldryn Keyne

Sir Caldryn Keyne is a wealthy elf from the Upper City of Baldur’s Gate, characterized by an impeccable, polished demeanor that often borders on arrogant. Raised in a world of "velvet privilege".

High-Born Disposition and Social Maneuvers

Caldryn often carries the habits of high society into the wilderness, such as serving wine from his private reserve and attempting to recruit a personal entourage even in Port Nyanzaru. His high charisma allows him to navigate social friction with ease; for example, in Session 2, he successfully defused a tense encounter with city guards by claiming his unauthorized recruitment attempts were merely a search for a "young intern for tutelage". He has also demonstrated a cunning use of his magical nature to bypass obstacles, such as in Session 3, when he "tricked" a Flaming Fist patrol by surrendering his magical pact weapon as collateral for an exploration charter, knowing he could simply re-summon it later.

Tutelage and Chivalric Ideals

Caldryn views himself as a mentor, frequently engaging in the "tutelage" of Scarloc Farmguard on the finer points of being a "chivalrous hero". Despite this self-appointed role as a paragon of valor, his early experiences in the jungle highlighted a frivolous nature that occasionally put the group at risk. In Session 3, following an ambush where he was nearly killed by dinosaurs, he displayed a rare moment of humility by kneeling in the mud to apologize to Mistral and vowing to be better prepared. Even when fumbling with his own gear, he maintained his focus on noble ritual, such as delivering a short eulogy and placing gold coins on the eyes of a fallen elf explorer to "pay the ferryman".

Divine Support and Crisis of Faith

In combat, Caldryn blends martial prowess with divine protection, often bolstering his allies with Lay on Hands, Shield of Faith, and Armor of Agathys. While he initially presented himself as a disciple of the god Tyr, his internal life is more complex. During a somber moment at a riverbank in Session 6, Caldryn made a startling confession to his companions, revealing a profound crisis of faith. He admitted that his divine powers do not actually originate from Tyr, but instead stem from his own inner self-belief.

Resourcefulness and Exploration

Caldryn’s keen eye for magical threats was instrumental during the party's trek through the jungle. In Session 4, he used magic to detect a glyph of warding trap on a statue of Ubtao. His willingness to participate in the "rider and carrier" protocol of the shrine demonstrated his commitment to the group's success, eventually leading to the recovery of the Alchemy Jug in Session 6.
