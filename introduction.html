Introduction

Campaign Overview

  • A death curse plaguing all of Toril emanates from the jungle island of Chult. Ancient ruins and hordes of undead fill the mostly unexplored and dangerous jungles. The mercantile city of Port Nyanzaru stands as a bastion of civilization on the northern edge of the jungle.
  • The tone of the game is fantasy Indiana Jones / The Mummy. Overall the jungle and especially the tomb are very dangerous and unforgiving; though some encounters will be light and even slightly 'wacky' fun.
  • The jungles of Chult are largely unexplored and very dangerous, filled with dinosaurs, strange goblins, and a lot of undead.
  • Wilderness survival is a key element of much of the game.
  • The peninsula's only city is Port Nyanzaru, a bustling port built on trade, filled with unique wonders, and run by powerful and enigmatic Merchant Princes.

Character Creation:

  • The Death Curse (in part) affects people who have been previously raised from the dead (They started wasting away). If you like, your character can have folks in their life who have been raised from the dead before the adventure starts; this might help motivate them to pursue the main external goal of the story (but that's not required!). The Death Curse started about 20 days before the start of the adventure and is widely talked about.
  • Characters can be from Chult, from the Sword Coast, or from anywhere. The current start idea is that you are all adventurer’s with a benefactor in Baldur’s Gate (I might change this based on your characters)
  • The game will probably begin in Baldur's Gate unless all / most of you decide your characters are from Chult.
  • Native Races from Chult are: Humans, Dwarves, Tabaxi and Aarakocra (Let me know if you want more details)
  • The Campaign is supposed to be difficult, so be as Meta in your character creation as you want, feel free to work together to synergize characters and mechanics
  • All Dnd 5.5e content published by WOTC is fair game (if it was reprinted for 2024, you have to use the new stuff 😉). Any 3rd party / Homebrew just run it by me.
  • Characters start at level 2, and get the basic starting equipment or the gold equivalent.
  • You may use point buy for your stats, or roll for your stats (4d6dl 6 times to create an array.)

Mechanics to Keep in Mind:

  • Revive magic doesn't work anymore.
  • Traveling
    • On the map of Chult, each hex measures 10 miles across. Characters moving at a normal pace can travel 1 hex per day on foot through coastal, jungle, mountain, swamp, or wasteland terrain. They can travel 2 hexes per day if they're traveling by canoe on a river or lake.
    • If characters move at a fast pace, they have a 50% chance to advance 1 additional hex that day. Characters moving at a fast pace take a penalty to their passive Wisdom (Perception) scores.
    • If characters set a slow pace, they have a 50% chance to advance 1 fewer hex that day. Characters moving at a slow pace can move stealthily. As long as they're not in the open, they can try to surprise or sneak by other creatures they encounter.
  • Dehydration
    • At the end of each day, any character who hasn't drunk at least 2 gallons of fresh water must succeed on a Constitution saving throw or suffer 1 level of exhaustion. The saving throw is made with disadvantage if the character is wearing medium armor, heavy armor, or heavy clothing. Characters traveling at a fast pace, instead of a normal or slow pace, take a penalty on their saving throws against dehydration.
    • A long rest will not restore exhaustion until the character consumes enough fresh water.
  • Weather
    • Chult is hot, humid, and rainy throughout the year. The temperature regularly climbs as high as 35 degrees C during the day and seldom falls below 20 degrees C even at night. A day without rain is rare, but rain varies from a steady mist to drenching downpours.
    • Visibility in heavy rain is limited to 50 yards. Beyond that distance, only Huge or larger objects can be distinguished. Missile weapon ranges are halved during rain.
