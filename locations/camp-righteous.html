Camp Righteous

Camp Righteous is an abandoned riverside outpost in the Chultan jungle that formerly served as a forward base for the Order of the Gauntlet. It is located on the edge of territory infested with undead.

History

The camp was established by the Order of the Gauntlet. Approximately two months prior to the adventurers' arrival, the outpost was overrun by undead. The few survivors of the slaughter retreated further upriver toward Camp Vengeance.

Current Status and Layout

Upon discovery by the party, the camp was found in a state of total wreckage, filled with moldy, tattered tents and permanent structures that had been burned to the ground. Notable features discovered at the site include:

  • The Great Statue: An 80-foot-tall stone statue carved to look like a man carrying a crocodile on his back dominates the northern ridge of the camp.
  • Animal Pens: A crude enclosure was found containing a young, panicked axe beak that had survived on worms since the camp's fall; it was subsequently released by the party.
  • Command and Soldiers' Tents: The remaining canvas structures are in various states of decay and rot.

House of the Crocodile

The House of the Crocodile is an ancient Chultan shrine built into the stony hillside behind the massive statue at Camp Righteous. Its entrance is a stone archway located directly between the feet of the Great Statue.

The Dangers and Traps

The shrine is notorious for its deadly magical and mechanical defenses, which the Order of the Gauntlet had abandoned their attempts to explore due to the lethality of the traps. Known hazards include:

  • Concealed Pit Trap: A pit hidden under mud and debris near the entrance.
  • Scything Blade Trap: Concealed wall niches that trigger blades to sweep across the corridor.
  • The Puzzle Floor: A terrace featuring a four-by-four grid of floor tiles. Stepping on the incorrect sequence triggers magical flames that blast from the walls.
  • The Puzzle Door: A wooden door that requires a specific sequence of tiles to be touched in order to open.

The Treasury

The innermost sanctum of the shrine, explored in Session 6, was a dusty, 40-foot-high vaulted chamber.

  • The Central Pillar: At the heart of the room stood a 30-foot-tall stone pillar featuring a narrow, dusty spiral staircase of 30 steps. The pillar was decorated with reliefs depicting the Man and the Crocodile.
  • Lightning Glyphs: The ascent was protected by magical Evocation glyphs on steps 3, 10, 17, and 23. These traps triggered 20-foot-radius spheres of lightning.
  • The Alchemy Jug: Resting at the peak of the pillar was the shrine's prize: a large, beautiful ceramic Alchemy Jug.
  • The Great Collapse: Upon claiming the artifact in Session 6, the shrine's final defense was activated. The ceiling began to collapse, raining heavy stones and bricks down on the party and burying the chamber in rubble.

The Legend

The imagery of the Great Statue and the solution to the shrine's traps are based on a classic Chultan fable. In the story, a Crocodile tricked a Man into carrying him on his back across the entire realm of humans.
