Session 5

The fifth session follows the expedition—Mistral Honeygleam, Sir Caldryn Keyne, Scarloc Farmguard, and Tidus Thalorien—as they reach the abandoned ruins of Camp Righteous and attempt to navigate a trap-riddled shrine.

Arrival at Camp Righteous

After a tiring day of travel through heavy downpours, the party arrived at Camp Righteous, an abandoned riverside outpost formerly held by the Order of the Gauntlet. The camp was a scene of wreckage, filled with moldy, tattered tents and burned permanent structures. Dominating the site was an 80-foot-tall stone statue carved to look like a man with a crocodile on his back.

Upon exploring the perimeter, the party encountered a Dragonborn named Kotumur, a member of the Order of the Gauntlet who had returned to assess the site. Kotumur confirmed that undead had overrun the camp two months prior, driving the survivors to retreat to Camp Vengeance further upriver.

During a search of the camp, the party discovered a young, panicked axe beak trapped in a crude animal pen. Using magic to speak with the bird, they learned it had survived on worms and scraps since the camp's fall. After providing it with rations, the party released the creature into the wild. The group then settled in for a long rest, with Scarloc Farmguard setting up the tents for the night.

Entering the House of the Crocodile

The following morning, the adventurers turned their attention to the stone archway between the feet of the massive statue, which led to a dark tunnel. Kotumur cautioned that the Order of the Gauntlet had previously abandoned their attempts to explore the shrine due to its deadly traps.

The party’s initial exploration was fraught with danger:

• Concealed Pit Trap: Near the entrance, the group identified a pit trap hidden under mud and debris. They eventually bypassed this by dragging one of the camp’s rowboats into the tunnel to serve as a makeshift bridge.

• Scything Blade Trap: Scarloc Farmguard triggered a trap that caused blades to sweep out from concealed wall niches, dealing him significant injury. The party jammed the mechanisms using metal tent spikes recovered from the camp.

• The Puzzle Floor: Further inside, the party encountered a terrace with a tiled floor in a four-by-four pattern. Stepping on the incorrect tiles triggered magical flames that blasted from the walls, causing further damage to several party members.

The Fable of the Man and Crocodile

Frustrated by the repeated traps, the party consulted Eku regarding the statue’s imagery. She recounted a classic Chultan fable in which a Crocodile tricked a Man into carrying him on his back across the entire realm of humans.

The party realized the legend served as a set of instructions: When they touched the door's tiles in the correct sequence while one character carried another, the door swung open to reveal the treasury

The Treasury

The final chamber was a dusty, 40-foot-high room littered with dinosaur bones. At the center stood a 30-foot-tall stone pillar with a narrow spiral staircase. At the top of the pillar sat the shrine's prize: a large, ceramic jug. Using magic, the party detected auras of conjuration and abjuration lingering around the pillar and the jug. The session ended as the party prepared to claim the artifact and depart the ruins.
